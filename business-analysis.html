Business Analysis

Case study, systems thinking, and communication

My business analysis work focuses on understanding problems from both a technical and organizational perspective. I am interested in how business needs, system design, stakeholder goals, and implementation choices connect.

This area of my work includes case analysis, structured problem solving, and applying concepts such as requirements thinking, SDLC planning, and communication of recommendations. Rather than focusing only on code, this work emphasizes how systems should be designed, improved, and explained.

I have used business analysis concepts in coursework and project work to evaluate current-state problems, identify practical solutions, and communicate recommendations clearly. This includes looking at how technical choices affect users, workflows, and long-term system value.

As I continue building this section, it will include case study examples, SDLC-related material, and communication-focused artifacts such as presentations and supporting write-ups.
